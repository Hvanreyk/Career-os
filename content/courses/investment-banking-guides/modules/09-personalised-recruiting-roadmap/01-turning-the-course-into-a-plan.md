Everything so far has built understanding. This module converts understanding into scheduled action — because a plan you don't execute against a calendar is just more reading.

## What your roadmap is built from

The Personalised Recruiting Roadmap (the workspace linked from your course overview) assembles a dated action plan from four inputs you've already generated:

1. **Your diagnostic and readiness score** — which of the six dimensions (industry understanding, technicals, recruiting-process knowledge, profile strength, networking, timeline) are weakest, and so where effort pays most.
2. **Your course progress** — lessons completed and module quiz results, which update your readiness as you learn.
3. **Your bank target list** — the firms and windows you're actually pursuing, from Module 8's tracker.
4. **Your timeline position** — how close you are to your next application window, from Module 6.

It turns these into four horizons — **this week, next 30 days, next 90 days, and before applications open** — each a short list of concrete, completable actions rather than vague intentions.

## Why the four-horizon structure works

The horizons defeat the two failure modes of student recruiting prep:

- **Paralysis** ("there's so much to do") — dissolved by a *this week* list of three or four specific actions you can start today.
- **Drift** ("I've got ages") — countered by a *before applications open* list that back-solves from your real deadline, exposing how little slack actually exists.

The near horizons are specific and action-heavy; the far horizons are milestones you steer towards. As deadlines approach, items migrate inward — a "next 90 days" networking goal becomes "this week: message two alumni".

## The plan is a hypothesis, not a contract

Your roadmap will be wrong in detail — a firm moves a date, a networking lead goes cold, a quiz reveals a weaker technical spot than expected. That's fine. A plan's value is that it makes your next action obvious and your progress visible, so you can adjust deliberately instead of drifting. Regenerate it as reality changes; the point is momentum with direction, not prediction.
