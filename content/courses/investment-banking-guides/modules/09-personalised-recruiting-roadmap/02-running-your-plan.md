A roadmap is only as good as your weekly execution of it. This final lesson is about the operating rhythm — and about keeping the whole pursuit in healthy proportion.

## A weekly operating rhythm

The students who convert plans into offers tend to run a simple weekly loop:

- **A weekly review (15 minutes).** Open your roadmap and tracker. What moved? What's due? What are this week's three or four concrete actions? Update statuses.
- **Protected blocks for the hard things.** Technical study and quality networking don't happen in gaps between lectures — schedule them like classes.
- **A steady networking drumbeat.** A couple of quality, personalised contacts per week compounds; a burst of fifty messages once does not (the Networking course covers the craft).
- **Application-readiness maintenance.** Keep your resume current and your market awareness ticking (fifteen minutes daily) so you're never rebuilding from cold when a window opens.

## Keep it in proportion

Two honest reminders as you finish the course:

**This course improves your odds; it doesn't guarantee an outcome.** Investment banking recruiting is competitive and partly outside your control — timing, cycle strength, chance. Do the work well, apply widely and early, and hold the results loosely. Effort you control; outcomes you influence.

**Investment banking is one good path, not the only worthy one.** If, having understood the job honestly (Module 1), it's not for you, that's a successful outcome of this course too. Consulting, other finance roles, corporate, tech and more offer excellent careers. The self-knowledge you've built transfers to any of them.

```callout
variant: warning
title: Protect the person doing the work
md: >-
  Recruiting stress is real and burnout produces exactly the sloppy
  applications and flat interviews you're trying to avoid. Sleep, exercise,
  relationships and time genuinely off the process aren't obstacles to
  performance — they're what sustains it across a months-long cycle. Pace
  yourself deliberately.
```

## Finishing the course

You've covered what investment banks do, how they're organised, the analyst reality, how deals work, the technical base, the Australian recruiting process, how to build a competitive profile, how to select and track firms, and how to turn it all into a plan. Concretely, you should now have:

- A readiness score (retake the diagnostic to see how it's moved).
- A started bank target list in your tracker.
- A personalised, dated roadmap to execute.

Retake the diagnostic to measure your progress, make sure your roadmap is generated, and start on this week's actions. The plan only works if you run it.
