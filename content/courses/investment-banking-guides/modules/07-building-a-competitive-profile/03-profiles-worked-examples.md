All profiles below are **fictional**, built to demonstrate patterns. For each pair, read the weak one first and diagnose it yourself before reading the verdict.

## Pair 1: the standard commerce student

```profile_example
strength: weak
title: "Fictional: Commerce student, penultimate year"
bullets:
  - "WAM 68 (Credit average), no explanation or trajectory shown"
  - "Member: Finance Society, Consulting Club, Economics Society, Wine Appreciation Society"
  - "Completed two 4-day virtual experience programs, listed as 'Investment Banking Experience'"
  - "Skills: Excel (advanced), financial modelling, leadership, teamwork"
  - No employment history listed at all
verdict: >-
  Every line is affiliation or claim, none is evidence. The Credit WAM
  needs offsetting strength that isn't there, "member" appears four times
  without a single outcome, virtual programs are overtitled as experience
  (a checkable embellishment), and claimed skills have no artefacts. A
  screener is done in fifteen seconds.
```

```profile_example
strength: strong
title: "Fictional: Commerce student, penultimate year"
bullets:
  - "WAM 78 (Distinction), D+ trend across finance majors; ATAR 98.4"
  - "Events Director, University Finance Society — ran 6 employer events incl. a 150-attendee banking panel; renewed 3 firm sponsorships worth $12k"
  - "Analyst, Student Managed Investment Fund — pitched 4 ASX stocks, 2 added to the $50k portfolio; run weekly sector update for 20 members"
  - "National case competition — top-8 finish of 60+ teams (M&A target evaluation)"
  - "Part-time retail (2.5 years, same employer) — shift supervisor from second year"
verdict: >-
  Same university, same degree — different evidence density. Every line is
  dated, quantified and verifiable; the retail job quietly proves
  durability; the SMIF and case comp lines will each carry a full
  interview conversation. This profile earns interviews at competitive
  firms.
```

## Pair 2: the non-finance background

```profile_example
strength: weak
title: "Fictional: Mechatronics engineering student, final year"
bullets:
  - "Strong engineering GPA, no finance coursework, activities or projects listed"
  - "Resume objective: 'Seeking to leverage my passion for finance in a challenging analyst role'"
  - "No explanation anywhere of why engineering → investment banking"
  - "Applying only to bulge-bracket graduate programs, two days before deadlines"
verdict: >-
  The pivot question — "why the switch, and what have you done about it?" —
  is completely unanswered. "Passion" appears as a claim with zero
  artefacts, and the late, narrow application strategy compounds the thin
  profile. Rejection is near-certain despite genuine ability.
```

```profile_example
strength: strong
title: "Fictional: Mechatronics engineering student, final year"
bullets:
  - "High Distinction average; thesis on optimisation under uncertainty (quant rigour, framed accessibly)"
  - "Self-directed: completed a modelling course, then built and documented a DCF + comps valuation of an ASX industrials company (write-up available on request)"
  - "Treasurer, university investment club — runs the $8k mock portfolio process"
  - "Winter off-cycle intern, 6-person M&A boutique — built company profiles and a precedent-transactions spread for a live mandate (obtained via alumni outreach)"
  - "Targets: graduate programs + 15 researched boutiques, tracked with priorities and follow-ups"
verdict: >-
  The pivot is pre-answered with artefacts: coursework gap closed
  visibly, real deal exposure earned through the networking track, and a
  two-pipeline application strategy. The engineering background now reads
  as a quantitative edge rather than a gap.
```

## The pattern behind every verdict

Strong profiles share four properties — check any line of your own resume against them:

1. **Evidenced** — outcomes, numbers, dates. Not "involved in", but "built/ran/won".
2. **Verifiable** — nothing you couldn't calmly discuss for five minutes.
3. **Durable** — at least one line proving sustained effort (a multi-year job, a long-held role).
4. **Directed** — the whole page answers "why finance" without saying the word "passion".
