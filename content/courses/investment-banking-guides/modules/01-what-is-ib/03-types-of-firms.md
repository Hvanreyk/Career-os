"Investment bank" covers everything from a 30,000-person global institution to a six-person advisory shop in Sydney. The tiers matter for your strategy: they recruit differently, staff deals differently and open different doors afterwards.

## The tiers

**Bulge brackets (BB)** are the global full-service investment banks — firms like Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, J.P. Morgan, UBS, Bank of America, Citi and Barclays. They advise on the largest transactions and can provide everything: M&A advice, equity and debt underwriting, lending, trading and research. In Australia they run lean local teams focused on major listed companies, which means small graduate intakes.

**Elite boutiques (EB)** are advisory-focused firms competing for the same large deals as bulge brackets, without big balance sheets — globally, firms such as Lazard and Rothschild & Co. Australia has an unusually strong independent scene: Barrenjoey and Jarden built full-scale local investment banks from launch in 2020, and long-standing independents like Gresham and Luminis Partners advise on top-end deals. Advisory-heavy work means analysts often get strong M&A exposure.

**Macquarie deserves its own line.** Macquarie Capital is the advisory/capital markets arm of Macquarie Group — Australia's home-grown global investment bank, consistently at the top of local league tables, with one of the largest graduate intakes in the market. Most students treat it alongside the bulge brackets in target lists.

**Mid-market firms** advise on smaller transactions (roughly tens to a few hundred million dollars) — international networks like Houlihan Lokey alongside local independents. Deal flow is often faster, and juniors typically get more responsibility per deal.

**Boutiques** are small advisory firms, often sector specialists or founded by senior bankers from larger houses. Hiring is ad hoc rather than through structured programs — a networking-driven path, and often the most realistic first entry point.

## Why the tier affects your experience

- **Deal exposure.** At a BB in Australia you might see fewer, larger deals; at a mid-market firm, more deals with more responsibility per deal. Both build strong skills — they are different.
- **Specialisation.** Big firms staff you into a product or coverage team; small firms make you a generalist by necessity.
- **Exit paths.** Large-cap PE recruiting has historically favoured analysts from BB/EB M&A and leveraged finance teams, but strong mid-market and boutique analysts move into PE, corporate development and funds constantly.
- **Recruiting mechanics.** BBs, Macquarie and the large independents run structured penultimate-year internship programs with fixed application windows. Boutiques hire when they need someone. Your recruiting plan (Module 6) has to cover both tracks.
