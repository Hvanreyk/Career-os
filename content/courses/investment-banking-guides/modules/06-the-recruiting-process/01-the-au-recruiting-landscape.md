## The main pipeline: penultimate-year summer internships

The central fact of Australian IB recruiting: **the summer internship in your penultimate (second-last) year is the main door.** Applications typically open **February–March** of your penultimate year and close **March–April**, for internships running **November–February**. Strong interns receive graduate return offers — at many firms, most of the graduate class.

Consequences worth internalising:

- Your "real" application deadline is earlier than you think: it's the summer-internship window, not graduate applications in final year.
- Preparation (resume, technicals, networking, market awareness) has to be substantially done *before* February of penultimate year.
- Rolling assessment means applying in week one beats applying at the deadline.

**Winter programs** suit students on non-standard timetables and add a second shot at the intern-to-grad pipeline. **Graduate programs** exist but are structurally harder: return offers fill many seats first. **Insight programs** (first/second year) are cheap options with real value — network, brand familiarity, sometimes fast-tracking into internship processes. **Off-cycle and boutique** hiring runs year-round and is covered in lesson three.

## Planning backwards from your window

If you're two-plus years from penultimate year: build grades, join societies, learn the industry (this course), start networking gently — you have the luxury of compounding. One year out: technicals, resume, market awareness, insight programs, systematic networking. Penultimate year: applications in week one, interview practice at full intensity. Final year or graduated: graduate applications *plus* the off-cycle/boutique track in parallel — structured programs alone are a thin pipeline at that point.
