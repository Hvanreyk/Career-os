Research is only useful once it becomes a managed list you act on. This lesson turns your firm research into a prioritised, tracked pipeline — using the Bank Target Tracker built into this course.

## Prioritise honestly, in tiers

Not every firm deserves equal effort. Sort your targets into three priorities:

- **Priority 1 (High):** strong fit, realistic given your profile, genuinely want it. These get your best applications, deepest research and most networking effort. Keep this list short — 4–8 firms — so "high priority" means something.
- **Priority 2 (Medium):** good fit but a reach, or solid firms you'd happily join but aren't top choices. Real applications, lighter networking.
- **Priority 3 (Low):** backups, longer shots, or firms you're still deciding about. Apply if capacity allows.

Honesty here is strategic, not modest: spreading equal effort across twenty firms produces twenty mediocre applications. Concentration on a realistic Priority 1 produces offers.

```callout
variant: warning
title: Realistic ≠ pessimistic
md: >-
  Priority tiers are about where your effort earns the most, not about
  talking yourself down. Include genuine reaches in Priority 1 if the fit
  is strong — just don't fill the whole tier with them. Balance ambition
  with a couple of well-matched firms where your profile is clearly
  competitive.
```

## What to track per firm

Your tracker holds a row per firm. Fill it as your research and outreach progress:

- **Division/team** you're targeting and why it fits.
- **Priority** (1–3) — revisit as you learn more.
- **Application window** — open and close dates (from the firm's careers page; verify, don't assume).
- **Status** — from "researching" through "networking", "applied", "interviewing", to an outcome.
- **Notes** — your one-sentence "why this firm", people you've spoken to, recent deals worth referencing, eligibility quirks.

The point isn't neatness — it's that in February of penultimate year, when six windows open in three weeks, you already have the research done and the dates in front of you. The tracker converts a panicked scramble into execution.

Two habits make the tracker pay off:

1. **Update status as you go** — a glance should tell you what needs action (a window opening, a follow-up due, an application to submit).
2. **Record every conversation** — who, when, what you learned, when to reconnect. This is where the Networking course and this tracker meet: relationships you don't record, you lose.
