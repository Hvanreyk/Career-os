Inside the investment banking division, teams are organised along two axes. Understanding the split is essential for interviews ("which group interests you and why?") and for reading job descriptions correctly.

## Two ways to slice the same work

**Coverage groups** own client relationships in a sector — Financial Institutions, Metals & Mining, Industrials, Consumer/Retail, Healthcare, Technology, Real Estate, Infrastructure. A coverage banker's job is to know their companies deeply, stay in front of management teams, and bring them ideas. When a deal happens, coverage often leads the relationship.

**Product groups** own transaction types across all sectors — M&A, Equity Capital Markets (ECM), Debt Capital Markets (DCM), Leveraged Finance, Restructuring. A product banker's job is deep execution expertise: an ECM banker prices and runs equity raisings whatever the industry.

On most deals the two work together: the coverage team brings the client and industry knowledge, the product team brings the transaction machinery.

## Why the distinction matters for you

- **Skill profile.** M&A and Leveraged Finance analysts typically do the heaviest financial modelling; ECM/DCM analysts live closer to live markets with faster, shorter transactions; coverage analysts build sector depth and client-facing materials.
- **Exit paths.** Private equity firms have historically recruited most heavily from M&A and LevFin because the modelling maps directly to buyout work. Sector-focused funds also value matching coverage experience. These are tendencies, not rules.
- **Lifestyle rhythm.** Market-facing products (ECM/DCM) cluster work around live deals and market windows — intense bursts, earlier mornings. M&A hours are driven by deal timelines and can run late for long stretches.
- **Interview answers.** "Why this division?" needs the vocabulary from this module. Saying you want M&A because it's "the most prestigious" lands badly; explaining you want end-to-end transaction exposure and heavy valuation work lands well.

## Sponsors coverage — a special case

One coverage group doesn't cover an industry at all: **Financial Sponsors** covers private equity firms. Sponsors teams serve clients who transact constantly — a single PE firm might buy and sell multiple companies a year — so the group sees exceptional deal flow and works closely with LevFin on buyout financing. For students interested in eventually working in PE, sponsors coverage is worth knowing about, though in Australia this often sits within broader coverage or M&A teams rather than as a standalone group.
