Five product groups execute most of the transactions you learned about in Module 1. For each one you should be able to say what the team does, what an analyst builds, and who the clients are.

## M&A (Mergers & Acquisitions)

Advises companies buying, selling or merging businesses. Analysts build valuation models (DCF, comparable companies, precedent transactions, accretion/dilution), draft process documents and management presentations, and support due diligence and negotiations. The work is long-cycle (months per deal), modelling-heavy and detail-intense — which is exactly why it's regarded as the strongest analyst training ground and the classic feeder into private equity.

## ECM (Equity Capital Markets)

Runs IPOs, follow-on raisings, block trades and hybrid instruments. ECM sits between the client and equity investors: structuring offers, building demand ("bookbuilding"), pricing and allocating. Analysts track market conditions daily, prepare pricing analyses and manage offer logistics. The rhythm is market-driven — quiet when windows are shut, flat-out when they're open. Australia's deep superannuation pool and listed resources sector make ECM a significant local business.

## DCM (Debt Capital Markets)

Helps clients issue bonds and manage their debt programs. Clients include the most frequent issuers anywhere: banks themselves, governments, and large corporates rolling over maturing debt. Deals are faster (days to weeks), more standardised and higher-volume than M&A. Analysts monitor rates and credit spreads, prepare pricing updates and support issuance execution. Less financial modelling, more markets fluency.

## Leveraged Finance (LevFin)

Arranges the debt that funds private equity buyouts and other high-yield borrowing. Sits between DCM and M&A: analysts model whether a company's cash flows can support a leveraged capital structure (debt capacity, covenants, returns) and work closely with sponsors teams. Strong LBO modelling exposure makes LevFin another well-worn path into PE. In Australia much of this activity involves bank lending and private credit rather than the large public high-yield bond market seen in the US.

## Restructuring

Advises companies in financial distress — and their creditors — on rescuing or reorganising the balance sheet: renegotiating debt, selling assets, raising emergency capital, or managing insolvency processes. It's counter-cyclical (busiest when markets are worst), analytically demanding, and smaller in Australia, where several independent firms and specialist practices lead the work.

## How to use this in interviews

You don't need to commit to a group as a student — most Australian programs are generalist anyway. What interviewers test is whether your stated interest is informed: if you say M&A, expect "walk me through a recent deal"; if you say ECM, expect "would you IPO in this market?". Pick one or two groups whose work genuinely appeals, learn their vocabulary, and connect them to deals you actually follow (Module 7 of the Market Awareness course builds this habit).
