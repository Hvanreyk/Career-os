## Why personalisation matters more than polish

A banker who receives a templated message can tell within one sentence.
Personalisation is not about clever phrasing — it is proof that you did five
minutes of real research and are contacting *this* person for a specific,
honest reason. That reason is almost always one of: shared university, a
similar path into finance, a specific piece of public work they did, or a
mutual connection who suggested you reach out.

```callout
variant: warning
title: The line you must not cross
md: >-
  Personalise only with facts you can verify and would be comfortable
  restating to their face. Never claim a mutual friend who did not suggest
  the contact, a connection to a deal you were not part of, or familiarity
  with their work you do not actually have. If in doubt, leave it out — a
  shorter, honest message beats a longer, invented one every time.
```

## Where to look, in order of usefulness

```table
caption: Research sources ranked by signal
headers: ["Source", "What it tells you", "Time to check"]
rows:
  - ["LinkedIn profile", "University, prior roles, how long in current role", "2 min"]
  - ["University alumni pages or societies", "Whether you share a genuine alumni connection", "2 min"]
  - ["Firm's own site or recent press", "What their team or desk is actually working on", "3 min"]
  - ["Public interviews or LinkedIn posts", "Their own words on what they value", "3 min"]
```

Five to ten minutes is enough. Research that takes an hour per contact will
not scale to the 2-3 junior and 1 senior contact you are aiming for per
target firm — and it is not necessary. One or two truthful, specific details
outperform an exhaustive dossier.

```profile_example
strength: weak
title: Generic opener
bullets:
  - "I came across your profile and was impressed by your career."
verdict: Says nothing specific — it could be sent to anyone at any bank, and the reader knows it.
```

```profile_example
strength: strong
title: Specific, truthful opener
bullets:
  - "I noticed you also studied Commerce at UNSW before moving into M&A — I'm in my penultimate year there now."
verdict: Concrete, verifiable, and gives the reader an obvious, comfortable reason to reply.
```

## Turning research into facts, not paragraphs

You do not need to write your personalisation as prose before you reach the
Message Lab. Capture two or three short, truthful facts — the shared
university, a specific role they held, a relevant public post — and let the
Message Lab combine them into a message. This also keeps the AI review
honest: it is only ever allowed to use the facts you explicitly give it.

```callout
variant: tip
title: Do this now
md: >-
  Before drafting outreach to a contact, add a short private note on their
  profile in the workspace with what you found — university, path, one
  relevant detail. When you open the **Message lab**, list the truthful
  facts you want the AI to use for personalisation. It will not invent
  anything beyond what you supply.
```
