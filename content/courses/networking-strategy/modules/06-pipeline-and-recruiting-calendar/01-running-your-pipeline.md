## Networking is a cadence, not a sprint

The students who build the strongest networks are rarely the ones who send
40 messages in one caffeinated weekend. They are the ones who send a handful
of good messages every week, for months, and keep every relationship
tracked well enough that nothing depends on memory.

A sustainable weekly target is **3-8 new outreach messages**, plus whatever
follow-ups and thank-yous are due. More than that, sustained for long, tends
to produce shallow research and generic messages — the opposite of what
Module 2 covered.

```callout
variant: note
title: Why the workspace suggests a range, not a fixed number
md: >-
  Your **Today** view calculates a weekly outreach target from your actual
  situation — it rises slightly when a recruiting window is open or when you
  have target firms with no coverage yet, and it never asks for more than
  8 in a week. The goal is a pace you can sustain for months, not a quota to
  hit once and abandon.
```

## Coordinate with the AU recruiting calendar

Australian IB recruiting follows a broadly predictable annual shape —
summer vacation programs, smaller winter programs, graduate applications for
final-year students, and off-cycle or boutique hiring that runs
year-round. **Exact dates move every cycle and differ by firm** — always
verify directly with each firm rather than relying on a general pattern.

```table
caption: How networking timing relates to each recruiting pathway
headers: ["Pathway", "Best time to be networking", "Why"]
rows:
  - ["Summer vacation programs", "Several months before applications typically open", "Relationships built early inform your applications and give you real context before you write them"]
  - ["Graduate programs", "Ongoing, but intensify once your penultimate-year outcomes are known", "Competition is harder since many seats go to returning interns"]
  - ["Off-cycle / boutique roles", "Continuously, all year", "These roles are rarely advertised — they are filled through networking and direct approaches"]
```

If you are not in your penultimate year, or missed a structured pipeline,
off-cycle and boutique opportunities are often the most realistic path in —
and they depend on networking almost entirely, since there is no portal to
apply through.

## Using coverage to decide where to invest next

By this point, you have a target map (Module 1), truthful personalisation
(Module 2), outreach and review habits (Module 3), a conversation approach
(Module 4), and a follow-up discipline (Module 5). The **Target map** and
**Pipeline** views in your workspace turn all of that into one operational
view: which firms have real coverage, which have none, and — combined with
each firm's typical application window — where your next week of effort
should go.

```callout
variant: tip
title: A simple weekly review
md: >-
  Once a week, open **Today**. Clear anything overdue first — it is usually
  a small number of quick actions. Then check the **Target map** for any
  firm with no coverage and a closing window inside the next couple of
  months, and make that firm your outreach priority for the week.
```

## From relationship to application

A strong network does not replace a strong application — it informs and
times it. Conversations tell you what a firm actually values, help you write
a more credible cover letter or interview answer, and occasionally lead to a
referral once genuinely earned. None of that removes the need to prepare
seriously for the actual recruiting process; it makes that preparation more
accurate.

```callout
variant: tip
title: Do this now
md: >-
  Review your **Target map** and pick one uncovered or thin firm to focus on
  this week. Set a realistic outreach target in the **Today** view, and keep
  the cadence going — a small, consistent effort over months outperforms any
  single week of intense outreach.
```
