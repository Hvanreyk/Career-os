## Relationships, not favours

Investment banking recruiting in Australia runs through structured graduate
programs, but a meaningful share of interviews and referrals still trace back
to a real conversation someone had months earlier. Networking is not asking
strangers for a job. It is building relationships with people who can share
information, context and — eventually, if it is earned — a referral.

Every conversation should be able to stand on its own even if nothing comes
of it afterwards. If the only value you can imagine from a coffee chat is
"they get me an interview," you are not ready to have it yet.

```callout
variant: note
title: The ethical line
md: >-
  It is reasonable to hope a relationship eventually helps your application.
  It is not reasonable to treat a person as a means to that end. Ask
  questions you are genuinely curious about. Say thank you whether or not
  anything comes of it. Never ask someone to lie, exaggerate, or refer you
  before they know your work.
```

## What a professional can actually give you

Before you reach out to anyone, be clear — for yourself — about what a
conversation can realistically provide at each stage of a relationship.

```table
caption: What each relationship stage can realistically offer
headers: ["Stage", "Realistic value", "Not yet appropriate"]
rows:
  - ["First contact", "Their own story, honest context about the role and the firm", "A referral or CV forward"]
  - ["One good conversation", "Advice on your application, introductions to peers", "A guaranteed interview"]
  - ["An ongoing relationship", "A referral, if they choose to offer one", "Demanding a referral because you asked nicely once"]
```

## Building a target map instead of a contact list

Random outreach to whoever appears in a LinkedIn search wastes your limited
attention and theirs. A target map starts from your actual bank targets —
the firms you are seriously pursuing — and asks a simple question for each
one: **who do I know, or need to know, there?**

```profile_example
strength: weak
title: Unfocused outreach
bullets:
  - Messaged 40 people across 15 firms with the same generic note
  - No sense of which firms were real priorities
  - Two weeks later, no memory of who had replied or who to follow up with
verdict: Volume without a map produces noise for the student and the professionals they contact.
```

```profile_example
strength: strong
title: Focused target-map approach
bullets:
  - Chose 5 realistic target firms based on fit, not prestige alone
  - Identified 2-3 people to reach at each firm across analyst and VP+ level
  - Tracked every contact's stage so nothing depended on memory
verdict: A small, deliberate list a student can actually sustain — and one the workspace can track for them.
```

Aim for **2-3 junior contacts (analyst or associate) and at least 1 senior
contact (VP or above)** per serious target firm. Juniors remember recruiting
vividly and are the most likely to reply; seniors carry more influence when a
referral eventually matters.
