## The reader has a sorting job

A first resume review is not a complete judgement of your potential. The reader is looking for evidence that you meet the role requirements, understand the work and can communicate clearly. Make the relevant facts easy to find rather than trying to sound impressive everywhere.

## Evidence beats claims

Education, employment, leadership and extracurricular activity become useful when they show what you actually did. A statement such as “excellent communicator” is hard to verify; a truthful example of presenting analysis to a committee gives the reader evidence.

## A practical scan order

Check that identity and contact details are current, education and dates are clear, the most relevant experience is prominent, and every bullet can be defended in an interview. Employer instructions always override generic guidance.
