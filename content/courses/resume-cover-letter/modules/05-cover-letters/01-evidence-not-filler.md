## Give each paragraph a job

A cover letter should identify the role, explain why the organisation and work are relevant to you, connect selected evidence from your experience, and close professionally. It should add reasoning rather than repeat every resume bullet.

## Make firm interest verifiable

Use public, current evidence such as the role description, stated team focus or a properly attributed conversation. Avoid generic prestige claims and do not pretend to have firm-specific knowledge you do not possess.

Remove filler that could be sent unchanged to any organisation. Check the firm name, role title, contact details and attachments before submitting.
